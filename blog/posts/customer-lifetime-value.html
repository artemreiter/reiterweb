What is Customer Lifetime Value?

Customer Lifetime Value is the total revenue you can expect from a single customer throughout your relationship. It shifts your focus from one-time transactions to long-term relationships.

LTV = Average Order Value √ó Purchase Frequency √ó Customer Lifespan

Why LTV Matters More Than Conversion Rate

Imagine two customers:

  • Customer A: Converts at 2% CAC, spends $50, never returns (LTV = $50)
  • Customer B: Converts at 5% CAC, spends $30/month for 2 years (LTV = $720)

Customer B has a lower conversion rate but is 14x more valuable. This is why LTV should drive your strategy.

How to Calculate LTV Accurately

Step 1: Calculate Average Order Value (AOV)

AOV = Total Revenue √∑ Number of Orders

Exclude outliers (orders 3x above average) to get a realistic number.

Step 2: Calculate Purchase Frequency

Purchase Frequency = Total Orders √∑ Unique Customers

Look at this over a specific time period‚Äîusually 12 months.

Step 3: Determine Customer Lifespan

Calculate the average time between first and last purchase. For subscription businesses, this is straightforward. For e-commerce, look at historical data.

Pro Tip: If you don't have enough historical data, use the inverse of your churn rate. If monthly churn is 5%, average lifespan = 20 months.

Advanced LTV Calculation (With Profit)

Revenue LTV is misleading. Use Profit LTV instead:

Profit LTV = (AOV √ó Gross Margin) √ó Frequency √ó Lifespan

This shows you exactly how much you can afford to spend on acquisition.

The LTV:CAC Ratio

The golden rule: LTV should be at least 3x your Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC).

  • LTV:CAC = 1:1 ‚Äî You're losing money on every customer
  • LTV:CAC = 3:1 ‚Äî Healthy, sustainable growth
  • LTV:CAC = 5:1+ ‚Äî You're under-investing in growth

How to Increase Customer LTV

1. Increase Average Order Value

Bundle products, offer free shipping thresholds, and use post-purchase upsells. Even a 10% increase in AOV compounds dramatically over time.

2. Increase Purchase Frequency

Email marketing, loyalty programs, and subscription options keep customers coming back. The goal is to become a habit, not just a one-time purchase.

3. Extend Customer Lifespan

Exceptional customer service, personalized experiences, and community building create emotional connections that last years, not months.
